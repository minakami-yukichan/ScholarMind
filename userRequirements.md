User Requirement & Project Definition

Target User Personas

1.  Graduate Students / PhD Candidates
Characteristics:  High-frequency  academic  paper  writing;  need  grammar   polishing, structure optimization, and formal academic expression.
Pain   Points:   Excessive   AI   rewriting,   untraceable   modifications,   concerns   about academic misconduct, unclear AI capability boundaries.

2. Young Researchers
Characteristics:  Journal  submission,  English  academic  writing,  pursuit  of  rigor  and accuracy.
Pain  Points:  Inaccurate AI-generated  content,  inability to  manually  revise  AI  results, difficulty in recovering from errors.

Core User Tasks

1.  Core End-to-End User Task
Paste / upload a paper paragraph → Trigger AI academic polishing → View modification reasons and confidence level → Manually overwrite AI results → Single-step / one-click undo → Export clean manuscript.

2. Task Success Criteria
1)    Complete AI polishing within 3 steps.
2)    View the reason for each AI modification.
3)    100% revert to the original text.
4)    Export a clean manuscript with no AI traces.

Project Scope & Boundaries

Key Constraints
1.  Usage Scenarios
Intended for use by graduate students, PhD candidates, and young researchers during academic paper writing, focusing on paragraph-level polishing, grammar optimization, and academic expression refinement.

2.  Device Limitations
Optimized for desktop web browsers; responsive design is not required. The prototype is only validated on desktop devices.

3. Technical Prerequisites
No back-end development or real AI model integration is needed. All AI functions are

simulated through a high-fidelity interactive prototype.

Design Assumptions
1.  Users have basic English academic writing skills and understand common academic norms.
2.  Users  prioritize  manual control over AI suggestions to ensure academic  rigor and authorship.
3.  Users need clear visibility of AI modification reasons and confidence levels to avoid inappropriate AI usage.

Excluded Requirements
-  Full academic paper generation
-  Data fabrication or fake research results
-  Literature search, download, or management
-  Automatic journal submission format conversion
-  Multi-user real-time collaboration
-  Cross-platform mobile adaptation
-  AI-based content originality or fact-checking
